How I Think

My frameworks for AI product decisions.

Senior PMs don't just execute — they have frameworks. Here's how I think about the problems most teams get wrong.

01

RAG vs Agent — When to use which

Most teams reach for agents first because they sound impressive. I start with the question: does this need to retrieve or does it need to act? RAG is the right call when you need grounded, accurate answers from your own data — support bots, knowledge bases, document Q&A. Agents are right when you need multi-step autonomous action — workflow automation, dynamic API orchestration, real-time decision loops. Mixing them up is where AI products fall apart.

RAGAgentsArchitecture
02

The Human-in-the-Loop boundary

Every AI product needs a clear boundary: what does AI decide, what does a human decide? I map this explicitly before writing a single PRD line. AI handles: scaffolding, test generation, UI iteration, documentation, boilerplate. Humans decide: architecture, security, UX choices, final QA, product direction. This isn't about distrust — it's about risk-proportionate design. Products that skip this step fail in production.

AI GovernanceRisk DesignUX
03

The 0→1 AI product checklist

Most AI products fail not because of bad models — but bad product decisions. Before any build, I validate: Is this a real user need or an AI use case looking for a problem? What's the data quality? Can the LLM hallucinate its way into a critical user path? What's the fallback when AI fails? Is the latency acceptable for this UX context? These 5 questions alone kill 70% of bad AI product ideas before they burn a sprint.
